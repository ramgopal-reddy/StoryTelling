The Chola Dynasty

Rajaraja I, the illustrious Chola emperor, is remembered for his military conquests, efficient administration, and the monumental Brihadisvara Temple.

Historical Milestones

985 CE
Rajaraja I Ascends the Throne

After the death of his brother Aditya II, Rajaraja I becomes king. Marks the beginning of one of the most powerful Chola reigns.

988 CE
Battle of Kandalur Salai

Rajaraja destroys the naval base at Kandalur Salai (Kerala). This victory marks the start of Chola naval dominance.

993 CE
Conquest of Northern Sri Lanka (Ila-mandalam)

Chola forces invade and capture Anuradhapura. Cholas establish Jananatha Mangalam in Polonnaruwa and Rajarajapura in Mantota.

1010 CE
Construction of Brihadisvara Temple

A remarkable feat of architecture built in Thanjavur, symbolizing the zenith of Chola artistic and political power.

1014 CE
Legacy Continues

Rajaraja I's reign ends, succeeded by Rajendra Chola I, continuing the empire's golden age and further expansion.

The Chola navy destroys the naval base at Kandalur Salai, asserting dominance over the western sea routes and coastal powers.

997 CE

Southern Conquests Begin

Rajaraja I launches successful campaigns against the Pandyas and Cheras, consolidating Chola authority in southern India.

Western Ganga Defeated

The Chola army defeats the Western Ganga dynasty in Gangavadi, expanding influence into modern-day Karnataka.

1003 CE

Naval Campaigns Intensify

Rajaraja I strengthens the Chola navy and begins overseas expeditions, preparing for expansion across Southeast Asia.

1007 CE

Tamil Saiva Revival

Rajaraja recovers and preserves ancient Tamil Saiva texts from decaying palm manuscripts, supporting temple culture and literature.
